FERRUCCIO LAMBORGHINI

Was born on April 28, 1916 in the small town of Cento, in the province of Ferrara. He was a wealthy manufacturer of tractors, air conditioning machines, and heating unit systems. It all started in 1963 with Ferruccio Lamborghini, who transformed a war relic into a tractor and from there created one of the most iconic automotive brands in the world. A trailblazer in every sense of the word, Lamborghini aimed to create sports cars that would become the best GTs of the time.

RIP 1916 - 1993

STHEPAN WINKELMANN

Winkelmann leaves Lamborghini after more than ten years with the brand. The 51-year-old executive became Lamborghini president and CEO in January 2005, and is credited with helping significantly elevate the brand’s reputation and sales. Last year, Lamborghini set an all-time sales record, of 3,245 cars sold globally. In his new role at Quattro GmbH, Winkelmann will in addition to R and RS models also oversee the Quattro Sport racing program, the Audi Exclusive customization arm, and the Audi Sport accessories collection.

CEO OF LAMBORGHINI 2005 - 2016
